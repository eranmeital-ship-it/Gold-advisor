Step by Step

How to open a Gold IRA or roll over a 401(k)

The process is the same whether you're funding with new contributions or moving an existing retirement account.

Open a self-directed IRA

Choose an IRS-approved custodian that handles alternative assets. A standard brokerage IRA can't hold physical metal.

Fund the account

Contribute cash up to the annual limit, or roll over funds from a 401(k), 403(b), TSP, or existing IRA — usually tax-free when done correctly.

Choose eligible metals

Buy gold of at least .995 fineness (plus the American Gold Eagle exception) through a dealer. The custodian executes the purchase.

Store at a depository

Metals ship directly to an IRS-approved depository in your name. You can't take personal possession without triggering a distribution.

Common Questions

Gold IRA FAQ

Can I store my Gold IRA metals at home?

No. The IRS requires that precious metals held in an IRA be kept by a qualified custodian in an approved depository. Schemes marketed as "home storage" or "checkbook LLC" Gold IRAs have repeatedly failed in Tax Court and can disqualify the entire account, triggering taxes and penalties.

What gold actually qualifies for an IRA?

Bullion must generally meet a minimum fineness of .995 (99.5% pure) and come from an approved refiner or mint. The American Gold Eagle is a notable exception — it's permitted at 91.67% purity because it's a U.S.-minted coin. Collectible and rare coins do not qualify.

How much can I contribute in 2026?

For 2026 the IRA contribution limit is $7,500 if you're under 50, with an additional $1,100 catch-up contribution if you're 50 or older. This is a combined limit across all your traditional and Roth IRAs. Rollovers from a 401(k) or other IRA don't count toward this limit.

Can I move my 401(k) into gold without a tax hit?

Usually yes, if it's done as a direct rollover or trustee-to-trustee transfer into a self-directed IRA. The funds move between institutions without passing through your hands, so no taxes or early-withdrawal penalties are triggered. Indirect rollovers have a 60-day window and stricter rules.
